Jeannie Joshi

Principal · Joshi Design LLC · Founded 2002 · Active practice: 2007–2022

Brand Strategy · Innovation · Creative Direction · Design-Technology

Making the Extraordinary Relatable. How do we create a visual, tactile, and imaginative experience that will invite a global audience to interact with this product?

Physical ExperienceRetail environments, brand activations, packaging, product graphics, strategic imagery, and production.
InterdisciplinaryBrand strategy, integrated identity systems, creative direction, and messaging.
Digital ExperienceWebsites, interfaces, content strategy, interaction design, and digital systems.

Founded and led a five-person strategic design practice in New York over fifteen years. Directed brand strategy, creative, and production across beauty, luxury fragrance, confectionery, housewares, toys, and retail, from concept through execution.

Partnered directly with Fortune 500 companies, founder-led brands, and startups, including L'Oréal across three divisions, Hasbro across three successive programs, and Godiva across eight years of seasonal collections. Approved supplier to L'Oréal and Johnson & Johnson. Multi-year relationships with senior decision-makers produced brand systems, original intellectual property, and consumer experiences that moved to market. In two cases, contributed to the conditions for acquisition.

Focus
Beauty

Daring expressions of color, with meticulous attention to image-making, developed for fashion, fragrance, cosmetics, body care, and consumer brands. The work was designed to attract the eye and suggest joy, serenity, and escape.

Corporate

Information translated for clarity and visual impact. Annual reports, brochures, websites, and public relations materials were designed to make facts easier to locate, understand, and use.

Kids

Learning environments designed as complete experiences. In classrooms, museums, retail, and product design, the work was shaped to capture attention, engage imagination, and support discovery.

Case studies with imagery. Forthcoming.

Selected Impact

Across beauty, fragrance, housewares, toys, retail, and personal care, the work consistently centered on building brand systems that could move from concept to market with clarity, distinctiveness, and staying power.

Global beauty, multicultural(L'Oréal)
Packaging, conceptual product development, brand expression, and strategic imagery across three divisions.

The work began with SoftSheen-Carson and expanded to Multicultural Beauty and the L'Oréal Kids World personal care range, developing category programs, visual systems, and packaging concepts used to evaluate direction.

Founder-led luxury fragrance(L'arganique Beauté)
Brand strategy, creative direction, campaign development, packaging, strategic imagery, digital flagship, and D2C integration across a six-year partnership.

Worked as the founder's right hand in shaping the brand from a private-label product into a business that achieved a successful acquisition. The work supported placements at the Emmy Awards and Critics' Choice Awards, and the hero fragrance won Best New Premium Fragrance at the Pure Beauty Global Awards three years in a row. Exclusive retail partnership with The Shops at Ritz-Carlton.

Successful acquisition
Premium confectionery(Godiva)
Seasonal packaging, original hand-drawn illustration, and gift collection development.

Created packaging and original artwork for seasonal gift collections across an eight-year partnership, helping refresh the brand's presentation for recurring holiday programs.

Family-owned housewares(Casabella)
Original artwork, surface pattern design, packaging, product graphics, website design, and strategic imagery.

Developed an original artist collection for the product line, including surface patterns, packaging systems, large-format product graphics, and extensions across cleaning tools. The collection replaced licensed Keith Haring graphics with brand-owned intellectual property. Featured in Graphic Design USA and Home Furnishings News. Worldwide retail rollout. Highest revenue in five years.

Acquired by Bradshaw International
Global toys + retail(Hasbro)
Brand strategy, retail environment concepts, modular fixtures, visual merchandising, and strategic imagery across three successive programs.

Designed modular retail environments and merchandising systems for Playskool, shown at the Hong Kong International Toy Fair and adopted for nationwide deployment. The work expanded across Tonka, Playskool, and a combined Vehicles Merchandising program that brought Tonka, Transformers, and Star Wars together within a single retail aisle.

National retail, digital(Kohl's)
Digital strategy, content architecture, interface design, and on-screen brand expression for an in-store touchscreen beauty experience.

Shaped a self-service digital experience for four prestige brands: American Beauty, Flirt, Good Skin, and Grassroots, uniting distinct visual identities within a shared navigational system. Deployed at the Short Hills flagship in New Jersey.

Global personal care(Carefree, Johnson & Johnson)
Packaging restage, brand system definition, strategic imagery, and brand book for a global product line.

Co-led a complete packaging restage with the in-house creative team, delivering updated pack design across the range and a brand book documenting the brand system and imagery. The work moved to market without consumer testing, based on the strength of the design.

Food + gifting(Pepperidge Farm)
Brand strategy, seasonal packaging, structural and graphic concepts, and production for holiday programs.

Created holiday gift packaging for the Signature Cookie Collection. The first season established the visual language for a single gift box; the second expanded to three SKUs and a cohesive line look across the holiday range.

Mass-market hair care, packaging: six concepts across four directions
Discovery
Design
—
Portfolio of options from subtle refinement to complete rebrand.
Challenge
The client had completed a brand equity study and needed it translated into packaging.
Approach
Six packaging concepts across four levels of ambition, from keeping what works to a complete rebrand.
System
Each direction tested for how it would hold up across the full product range.
Outcome
Analyzed the equity research and competitive landscape to understand where the brand had room to evolve. Framed the options so the client could see the tradeoffs.

System
Four brand experiences inside one system, each recognizably different.

Children's toy brand, modular retail environment: nationwide deployment
Discovery
Design
Deliver
Retail experience for kids and the adults buying for them.
Challenge
The brand needed a retail experience that worked for both kids and the adults buying for them.
Approach
Two concepts for a modular environment using sustainable materials and interactive elements.
System
Designed so the client's own fabrication partners could build it and roll it out from one store to every store.
Outcome
Studied the brand's priorities and target audiences. Designed a physical environment around the brand's learning-through-play positioning. Delivered fully engineered blueprints for buildout.
Three global toy franchises, retail aisle merchandising
Discovery
Design
Deliver
Three progressive waves from incremental to transformational.
Challenge
Three brands with different audiences and identities had to coexist in one retail aisle without drowning each other out.
Approach
Three progressive waves. First: improve what's there. Second: add physical presence with dimensional signage and floor elements. Third: bring in screens, sound, and lighting.
System
Each wave built on the one before, giving the client a clear choice of how far to invest.
Outcome
Assessed each brand's identity and audience. Developed an aisle system where all three had clear presence without competing.
Prestige nail care, brand redesign roadmap: 68 SKUs across nail lacquers, treatments, tools, and accessories
Discovery
—
—
Roadmap and framework for a future full-line redesign.
Challenge
Prestige nail and body care line spanning lacquers, treatments, tools, and accessories across specialty retail, department stores, and home shopping.
Approach
Competitive and retail research. Three directions exploring how the brand could look and feel as a unified collection.
System
Mapped the full 68-SKU assortment as one brand system so the client could see how a redesign would work across every product type before committing.
Outcome
Delivered a roadmap covering phasing, manufacturer considerations, prototypes, brand guidelines framework, and a communications outline as inputs for the eventual redesign.
